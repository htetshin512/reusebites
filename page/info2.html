How To Reduce It

Reducing food waste starts with planning your meals so you only buy what you need and avoid unnecessary purchases. It's also important to store food properly to keep it fresh for longer, using containers and labels when needed. Many people throw away food too early, so it's helpful to understand expiration labels "best before" means it’s still safe, just not at peak quality. One of the easiest ways to cut waste is to use leftovers creatively, turning extra food into new meals or freezing it for later. You can also reduce waste by serving smaller portions and only taking more if you're still hungry. Instead of tossing scraps, try composting food waste to help the environment. If you have too much food, consider donating extra food to those in need. And don’t forget “ugly” fruits and vegetables are just as nutritious and tasty, so they shouldn't be thrown away just because they look different. Making these small changes can help protect the environment and build a more sustainable future.
